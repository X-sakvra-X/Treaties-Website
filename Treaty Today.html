Why Do We Still Talk About Treaty 1 Today?

Treaty 1 is not just history—it is still important today!

  • 🤝 People still receive treaty payments every year as part of the agreement.
  • 🎓 We all live on Treaty 1 land, and it is important to learn about its history.
  • 🏘️ First Nations families still live on reserves that were promised in Treaty 1.
  • 💬 First Nations leaders still work hard to make sure that the treaty promises are honored.

Learning about Treaty 1 helps us understand that we must respect each other and take care of the land together.

How Can We Honor Treaty 1?

Everyone who lives in Treaty 1 territory has a role to play in honoring the treaty. Here are some ways we can do this:

  • 🌿 Learn about First Nations history and culture.
  • 📚 Read books and listen to stories from Indigenous Elders.
  • 🗣️ Talk about treaties and why they matter.
  • 🏞️ Respect the land and take care of nature.
  • 🖐️ Treat First Nations people with kindness and fairness.

When we learn, listen, and respect Treaty 1, we help make sure that the promises made long ago are never forgotten.

Treaty 1: A Promise for the Future

When Treaty 1 was signed in 1871, it was meant to be a promise that would last forever.

Even though some promises were broken, First Nations people never forgot what was promised to them.
